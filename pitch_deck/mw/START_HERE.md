## ðŸŽ¯ Key Strategies for Success

### 1. **Show Your Thought Process**
Don't just state answers. Walk them through your reasoning:
- "Let me think through this systematically..."
- "There are trade-offs here: X vs. Y..."
- "I'd approach this by first understanding..."

### 2. **Use Concrete Examples**
Never speak in abstractions. Always use:
- Specific code snippets
- Actual numbers (latency, accuracy, cost)
- Real bugs you encountered and fixed
- Example state transitions

### 3. **Be Honest About Gaps**
Admitting what you don't know shows maturity:
- "I haven't implemented that, but here's how I'd approach it..."
- "That's a gap I'd address before production..."
- "I don't know that specific technology, but I've used similar..."

### 4. **Connect to Their Domain**
Marshall Wace is quantitative trading. Draw parallels:
- "In trading, latency is critical - similar to our sensor processing..."
- "Your systems probably need strong idempotency like ours..."
- "Market data is probably noisy like our sensor data..."

### 5. **Show Curiosity**
Ask questions about their systems:
- "How do you handle X in your environment?"
- "What latency requirements do trading systems have?"
- "I'd love to learn how you approach Y..."
